About Chromosome 3

Chromosome 3 is one of the largest human chromosomes with almost 200 million pairs that represents almost 7 percent of the DNA in cells. Chromosome 3 also contains between 1,100 and 1,500 genes, including gene clusters encoding for olfactory receptors, genes involved in DNA repair such as MLH1 gene (mapped to 3p21.3), and more than 500 disease loci have been mapped to chromosome 3.

Many loci are involved in multiple human cancers such as FHIT gene (mapped to 3p14.2), that contains the fragile site locus FRA3B, involved in half of all oesophageal, stomach and colon carcinomas.

Other medically relevant such as CCR5 gene (mapped to 3p21.31) that encodes for chemokine receptors and the defective alleles are associated with HIV resistance.

About our web

The knowledge about chromosome 3 provides a rich resource for understanding the molecular basis of human variation and disease.

The goal of our web is to provide some online bioinformatics tools to access chromosome 3 sequence and get some functional information, and includes:

  • Search the database to find an entry based on gene identifier, protein product names, Genbank accession, or chromosomal location.

  • View a summary list of all the gene identifiers, protein product names, Genbank accession and chromosomal location.

  • View the following information for a particular entry: the complete DNA sequence with the coding regions highlighted, the amino acid sequence displayed with the coding DNA sequence, codon usage frequencies within the coding region as well as the overall codon usage information for all sequences in the database and identify sticky-end restriction enzymes in the genomic DNA.
